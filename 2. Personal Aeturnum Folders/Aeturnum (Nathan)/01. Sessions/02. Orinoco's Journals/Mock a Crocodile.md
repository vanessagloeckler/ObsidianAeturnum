<p style="text-align:center;">
It is Only Safe to Mock A Crocodile<br>

After You Have Crossed the River <br>

(Initiation)
</p>
I have tried today, for the first time, to draw meaning from the stars on my pitcher. To my great surprise, I recognized two of the symbols that appeared. The first was *Piranha*, a warning of blood in the water, and the second was *Scarlet Ibis*, a symbol of rejuvenation. These are both constellations that I appear to be bound to, and draw my power from. The Piranha makes my offensive magicks more deadly, while the Scarlet Ibis shows me how best to channel my healing powers into my friends. The third constellation was unknown to me in the morning, but by the end of the day both its shape and meaning became very clear.

Last night I dreamt of Aernoc Palace again. I was in those dreadful coffin tanks that convert the forsaken into the damned. I awoke to screaming - not my own, thankfully, but that of my new companions, the Wayfarers. I approached Brick, a rather fetching lady dwarf with biceps bigger than my thighs. I felt the full force of those arms as she instinctively cracked me in the jaw, in a bid to defend herself from her own nightmare. Elsewhere, Beetle found themself trampled by another new ally, a large human by the name of Elias Mulch - aptly named as he looks and smells as though he had just crawled out of a bog. 

Scarlet arrived in our tent then, clearly aggravated by whatever covert mission she had been on over the past few days. She proceeded to insult and mock us for our memory loss, and the associated nightmares. A bizarre position to take I thought, and said as much, given that it was her failures as a commander that resulted in both. If she feels any remorse for this, she did not show it, and instructed us to follow her. We were to hunt bloodkin, it would seem, in order to demonstrate to the Guild Master that we are fit to return to active duty.

There was to be seven of us in the hunting party, much like our escape from Tisis - what's the significance of that, I wonder? Myself, Brick, Elias Mulch, and the unsettling Beetle. Reassuringly, Milo would be with us as well, and bringing up the rear was another suspect wayfarer by the name of Loup Garou. I sense the wrath of nature in him, as I do Elias Mulch, but there's something darker in Loup, darker even than his connection to Agelamorn... Our final companion is Cora, a Tiefling cleric of some severe domain, I should think. Her skin is remarkable, though. Much like my pitcher, it is covered in patterns of stars and constellations. I have tried comparing my jug to her skin, but to no avail so far. 

Prior to the hunt, Scarlet deposited us into the hands of Astra - another creature with bizarre skin, this one glowing a faint blue tone - who was to be our quartermaster. Astra lives in the Armoury, and provided us with an explanation of these Hunter's Tools that we wear. None of them seem particularly useful to me, but I shall carry the Crucible Shield, at least. Despite their scattered nature, Astra was honestly the most forthcoming person I've met so far. They very kindly told us that we are setting out not only to slay Agelamorn, but also the 'Outer God of Blood' that she serves... They were vague on the specifics, but was kind enough at least to acknowledge the difficulty of such a challenge.

The hunt began then, with a horse ride and a little climbing. Mulch and I both overcame the obstacle by turning into giant lizards - his dull brown a lovely accompaniment to my glorious red. Scarlet then led us into an old temple, stunning in its nondescriptiveness. As we approached, we heard the cry of a familiar voice, though I could not place it. When we emerged into what must have been the central worship chamber, we encountered a horrific sight. A bizarre harlequin, clearly a servant of Agelamorn, torturing some of our scouts. The only survivor was a woman named Elaria, though she was clearly not long for this world, and far beyond my healing abilities. Just like Theolyn...

Nonetheless, we did battle with the harlequin and their forces, including the reanimated bodies of our own comrades. Beetle, the fool, chose to engage the harlequin directly, having seen though their invisibility. The harlequin quickly dispatched them, even despite my healing and Milo's attempts at rescue. Upon their death, Beetle was consumed by a sanguine pool, then suddenly reappeared in a gory geyser of blood. I'd love to say that it was the most unsettling thing I've ever seen Beetle do... I'd love to say that it was even the most unsettling thing I've seen Beetle do *today.* I didn't notice it at the time, but when I examined them later, it seemed as though their teeth had grown sharper...

Despite their bravado, the harlequin was quickly overwhelmed by our assaults. They fled, for real this time, and teleported away. According to the magicks of Beetle and Mulch, they had gone at least 10 miles away from here. Frustrating for us, but good for Adrestia, I suppose. We were commended, and informed that we were now declared fit for active duty, whatever that means. 

I studied my pitcher again this evening, just moments ago, and came to recognise a shape within the unknown constellation. I shall name it the Harlequin's Mask, and it will embody the pointless havoc of the sadistic creature we met this day.
