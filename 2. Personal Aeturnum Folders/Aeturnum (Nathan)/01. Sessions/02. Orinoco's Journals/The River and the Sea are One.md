<p style="text-align:center;">
For Life and Death are One<br>

As the River and the Sea are One<br>

(Anathema)
</p>

To view my curseblood as a blessing seems a twisted perspective, yet what I saw today makes me understand just how much worse I could have it. Even if I had known what my stars were trying to warn me of this morning, I could not have prepared myself.

We were sent to Brigmore Commons by request of a speaker. It may have been a nice place, once. Quaint. But now... The dead and dying line the streets. They are nothing more than statues now, turned to stone by the Worldwound's petrification. There was nothing I could do. My magicks are but a candle's flame against a blizzard. I could not even tell which were dead and which were simply dying, I had to rely on Abyssian. He reached into the minds of the dying, and identified which had already passed. Perhaps I distrusted him too quickly when we met in Tisis... We must have been in the same squad for a reason, after all.

There are those who are fighting the petrification curse. I met two, today. The first, Aline. Proprietress of the former tavern *Sun Above Water*, who has converted her home and livelihood into a care centre. She works all day with almost no supplies, against an unknowable force that kills all that it touches, yet she does not give up hope or fall into despair, as I did in mere minutes. The second was a vampire, would you believe, and a former member of Agelamorn's court, no less. Mina researches the petrification, and has made some minor progress, at least. Ether seems to be the key. It is capable of delaying the petrification, as well as my own curseblood, and may possibly even suppress vampirism itself? Mina was unsure, but even a glimmer of hope can light up a dark room.

But she was not safe. As we found her, she was abducted by Haera's forces - two ghastly necrotic machines. There was something organic within them, of that I am certain, but they were mostly mechanical in nature. I wonder if Henrietta is of a similar nature? I fear I am happier not knowing... Abyssian and I were able to rescue Mina, with the assistance of some delightful new Wayfarers. Two of them have a touch of the divine about them, much like me. A tiny creature named Ember who, despite his stature, delivered some lethal blows. Lugos, on the other hand, seems a sharp mind but perfectly passive.

Lugos's companion Zanya is a brave soul, and that nearly got her killed. Nonetheless, I admire her pragmatic nature - she had no qualms blasting me and Abyssian, and even our commanding officer, if it meant taking down one of Agelamorn's minions. Our commander on this mission, one Silas Reiner, does not inspire confidence thus far, but he is at least more respectable to our plight than Scarlet. Not bad on the eyes, either... The last of our squad was an intimidating goliath named Shade. A descendant of the cloud giants, with an obsidian axe that he wields with deadly force. I hope to have him at my back in future missions.

Mina was saved, and was remarkably calm about having a sword rammed through her stomach. We begged her to come with us, to join the Wayfarers and lend us her research and her knowledge on Agelamorn. She despises the vampiric queen as much as we do, yet she refused. Fear, I believe, and I don't blame her. Lugos encouraged her to find a new place to hide, given that she was found here. I hope we see her again, and that she has made more progress in her research when we do.

There was a nameless girl in the *Sun Above Water*, almost completely turned to stone, laid in bed next to a boy. Abyssian read her mind and learned that her brothers name was Luke, and that her last thoughts would be of him. Luke was already gone. I do not know your name, sweet child, but I will place you and your brother into my nights sky, and I will burn Haera Agelamorn till she is naught but dust, to be thrown through that abhorrent gash before I slam it shut. Until then, rest, my Cherubs.
