<p style="text-align:center;">
The Axe Forgets<br>

The Tree Remembers <br>

(Requiem)
</p>

I don't know who I am, but I am covered in blood.

My name is Orinoco, of that much I am sure. It means 'the place we go paddling together', that much I can remember. I see flashes of a woman that I'm sure is my mother, walking me along a river as a child, as she tells me my namesake in gnomish singsong, though gnomish we are not. I can speak it, and also the common tongue, as do all of my unknown companions. I speak elvish as well, it seems. One of my companions is an elf - her name is Wally and she wields powerful magicks, but she does not know me. Or at least, she does not know herself and therefore can't know me. 

We are accompanied by two other spellcasters, both of whom give me the creeps, quite frankly. One is a strange wizard named Beetle, seemingly for his stature. The other is a jet-black dragonborn named Abyssian. His scales are almost as unsettling as that foul void in the sky. Two more companions are powerful warriors; Milo the small and nimble halfling, and Marcello Gangrene, who has a capacity to defy death even beyond that of other orcs. Our final amnesiac companion is Valkath Stoneheart Gathynakari, a shapeshifter like me, and a force to be reckoned with. In battle, the seven of us had a certain familiarity, yet none of us can recall who we were before we woke up in the harvest chamber. 

However, those that rescued us have told us who we are, or at least who we are to them. We are Wayfarers, members of a guild from a city called Adrestia. The name rings a bell, at least. I serve in this guild under the direction of a rather fetching human woman called Scarlet - a code name, I should hope, for what parent would name a child after the colour of their own hair? She was incompetent enough a leader to cause us all to be captured, yet competent enough and brave to boot, to rescue us before we were... 'converted'.

Scarlet in turn serves under our Guild Master, Elaric Valent, who sits across from me in the wagon as I write this, having kindly rummaged up this lovely notebook! He seemed to know that I'd need one. He seems to know a lot... He told us that due to what we experienced in the harvest chamber, we are now 'cursedblood', whatever that should mean. He told us of our enemy, one Haera Agelamorn, the vampiric queen of Tisis, and how we are seemingly linked to her, bound to her by blood. There is undoubtedly some presence in my mind, though it weakens as we travel away from Tisis.

The Worldwound... I do not know who I am, but I know that sealing that abominable void must be my goal. It blocks out the very stars themselves. Elaric tells me that I am a druid, and that I draw my power from the stars, and I must admit I feel a certain pull to them as we move further away from Agelamorn's profanity. Elaric assisted me in a ritual to conjure back my spellcasting focus - the most beautiful water pitcher, covered in a map of constellations. Some I know - Piranha, Scarlet Ibis, Arrau Turtle - but most I don't remember, if I ever knew them to begin with. Every time I look I see new ones. There must be hundreds in total.

There is one sign that speaks to me. In it I see a person, with what I once may have considered wings? But now all I can see is something altogether darker... Theolyn Esmond was his name. He knew me, as Scarlet and Elaric know me. He was my friend, I think, and I killed him. It was a mercy, there was no undoing what Agelamorn had done, but it was me that killed him all the same. Theolyn... I will write your name into the constellations and paint you across the sky, my sweet Angelus.
