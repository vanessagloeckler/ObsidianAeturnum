# NOCTURNE: NARRATIVE OVERVIEW

Having driven out the [[03. Elements from Aeturnum 1|Cult of the Formless Mind]], the people of [[2. Personal Aeturnum Folders/Aeturnum (Vanessa)/Bloodtide/Adrestia]] celebrated their hard-won freedom. Unaware that the [[2. Personal Aeturnum Folders/Aeturnum (Vanessa)/Factions/The Nine]] had fallen silent for the first time in centuries. In the cloistered halls of Highgarden Court, the Speakers gathered to hear their final decree - but were left only with a grim promise of the ruin that awaits. 

‘The Herald is coming. Fear the night.’ 

Even they were not prepared when the heavens were carved open. The [[Worldwound]] emerged in the sky above, a screaming fissure in reality that soon drowned the Isles in cursed light. Life itself began to slow, the changes barely perceptible until it was much too late. The first victims of the [[Petrification Curse]] died in agony, as they were quarantined and trapped in a prison of their own crumbling flesh. The city's Occultists soon determined that this curse, and the Worldwound itself, were the results of vampiric ritual magic. Born from the courts of [[Tisis]], and their immortal Queen - [[Haera Agelamorn]]. Only she could possess the power needed to break the natural order of the world. And only she could be deranged enough to accept the consequences.

Without intervention, all life would soon be extinguished and devoured to fuel Haera's designs. The Guilds of Adrestia united, arming themselves with the most powerful relics and weapons of war gathered from the ashes of the Old World. Their greatest champions would lead the expedition into Tisis, and mount an all-out attack on Haera's palace. 

What awaited them was the newly ascended, blood-drunk Queen of Tisis at the very height of her power. Bathed in the pulsating glow of the Worldwound and swollen with the crimson blessings of her Fell God. Only by reducing her to ash, could this slow but inevitable extinction be averted.

[[06. Timeline|Thirty years later]], you assume the role of Hunters. Elite members of the [[Wayfarer’s Guild]], and humanity’s last hope. You have been tasked with leading an expedition across the [[05. Glossary of Minor Terms|Red Isles]], into the heart of the vampiric continent, to slay [[The Outer God of Blood]]’s chosen consort and seal the Worldwound. Gathering allies and resources to aid your cause as you carve a path through this endless night. 

Aeturnum II is an action-horror experience, with psychological horror elements. Punctuated by the blistering wails of demonically-possessed industrial machinery and your own accursed blood.

# THE WAYFARER’S CONVOY

As members of the Wayfarers Guild, you will be tasked with overseeing and upgrading an armoured convoy of wagons that have been specially outfitted to repel the vampiric horde. This is the central mechanical pillar that will drive your journey in Campaign II. 

The convoy serves as a homebase for you and all the allies you will gather on your journey. By gathering resources from the frontier and upgrading the convoy, you will gain access to a variety of unique upgrades. 

It will be up to you to decide which projects and facilities the convoy pursues. Every month’s Narrative Event will see a new Hunter’s Council elected, with a player from every table chosen to represent the Guild. 

As your journey begins, the Council will only be able to select two active projects per month - but upgrading the convoy will allow you to research and progress more simultaneously over time. 

Every Quest you complete will reward the convoy with resources, which will automatically be split and contributed towards that month’s active projects. When a project requires a rarer resource (or sacrifice) to complete, this choice will be left to the Hunter’s Council. 

Once unlocked, all players can freely access these upgrades as they wish. This is the largest mechanical addition to Campaign II, and will be supported by a substantial overhaul of the Aeturnum site portal. 

The Wayfarer’s Convoy page will allow you to view the convoy’s current resources, track ongoing projects, and browse all completed and available upgrades. In addition to this, there will be a journal with current quest leads and codex of important discoveries made throughout your journey.

The Wayfarer’s Convoy is core to the upgrade and progression overhauls that will replace our existing Boon system. It is intended to provide an experience where every Quest completed meaningfully contributes towards new upgrades; and that players who cannot attend frequent sessions do not feel unfairly disadvantaged and locked-out of these rewards.
# HUNTER’S ARMAMENTS AND LEVEL PROGRESSION 

Hunters are amongst the most elite members of the Wayfarer’s Guild, tasked with scouting ahead of the main convoy and rooting out any threats that might lie in wait. 

Upon their initiation, every Hunter is issued their bracer. A crucial tool for combatting the undead scourge, and a badge of honour worn proudly by those deemed fit to lead the expedition. 

A [[Technology|Hunter’s bracer]] is an elegant leather vambrace, plated with lightweight but extremely durable felscorite steel. Each bracer is adorned with the Wayfarer’s seal, and a lattice of protective runes to safeguard the elaborate mechanisms concealed beneath. 

Mountings on the bracer’s outer plating allow numerous specialised tools to be equipped. These tools are fueled by highly pressured etheric canisters - emitting plumes of steam and arcane particulates as their clockwork machinery unfurls. 

Etheric cells are a precious commodity, forged in the churning depths of the [[05. Glossary of Minor Terms|Atropos Refinery]]. Only a handful are afforded per hunt, but even this is enough to turn the tide in the hands of a skilled Hunter. Those that live long enough to hone their craft will often modify their tools, using salvaged resources found in the field. 

Mastering these tools will be essential to combatting the [[Curseblood Plague]], and enduring the endless night its Queen has ushered forth. 

Hunter’s Tools are unique and powerful armaments that you will be able to unlock and upgrade throughout Campaign II. When you complete the Prologue, you will receive a physical Hunter’s Journal that is used to level up your character and upgrade your tools.

Hunter’s tools are tied to your individual character progression, with four being immediately available in the Hunter’s Journal. Whenever you complete a quest, you will receive two Experience Points which will be stamped into your journal. One point will go towards your level progression, and the other will go towards upgrading your Hunter’s Tools. 

Hunters Tools will be selected alongside your equipment loadout before embarking upon any expedition. Once you have fully upgraded a tool - you will gain Mastery with it. You can equip and wield any two tools together if you have mastered them, as you are issued a second bracer to reflect your status as a veteran Hunter. 

Some tools have unique variants that can be equipped and upgraded by completing the respective upgrade tree. Variants must be declared and equipped before beginning play, and cannot be freely switched mid-expedition. 

These variants allow you to further customize and hone your build. For example - the Hunter’s Autobow is an ornate wrist mounted crossbow. Consecrated with divine wards and capable of firing rapid bursts of silvered bolts. 

The Ravager Autobow variant is well suited to high-risk, aggressive DEX builds. With a reduced range, but the ability to stack the lethal Haemorrhage condition with its serrated bolts and slow your foes. The Elementalist’s Autobow excels in the hands of hybrid-casters, trading raw damage output for the ability to absorb incoming elemental damage. Infusing your own bolts with this raw arcane energy, and allowing you to exploit the elemental weaknesses of your foes. 

As your journey continues across the Isles and you upgrade your convoy, you will have the potential to unlock new Hunter’s Tools for yourself and the expedition.
# THE ARMOURY 

The Wayfarer’s Armoury is a unique convoy facility, overseen by one of your more enigmatic allies… 

The Armoury provides an arsenal of weapons, armour and equipment that can be freely switched between sessions, allowing players to freely experiment with builds and tweak their loadout before embarking upon an expedition. 

To begin with the Armoury will stock a variety of basic armaments that would be available to new adventurers, either given through your starting gear or purchased with your starting GP. As such, all gear available to you in Campaign I following our character creation guidelines will still be accessible. 

The Armoury will carry an assortment of tools, but you will only be able to take them into the field if you are proficient with their use. 

As you upgrade the armoury and complete requests for its owner, you will be able to access rarer armaments honed to repel the curseblood plague. 

All players will be able to access and browse the Armoury as part of the new Wayfarer’s Convoy page. When combined with the other new convoy mechanics, we intend for the Armoury to provide players with a deeper sense of progression and customization even after hitting the level-cap.
# AN UNLIKELY ALLIANCE: GATHERING ALLIES

Slaying a blood-drunk God is no simple feat. To end Haera’s immortal reign, you will need to gather allies to your cause. 

Every month will provide the opportunity to recruit new characters to join your expedition. Allies can provide the convoy with unique upgrades and abilities, or join you in the field if called upon to even the odds. 

Fighting alongside allies is not without peril, as any character you recruit may be killed or infected with the curseblood plague. 

Reputation with your allies is persistently tracked across tables - if you slight them or refuse their requests, there is every chance they might leave the expedition or betray you outright. 

Not every ally you recruit can be trusted. They will each harbour their own hidden motivations, it will be up to you to decide who to protect, or to cast out into the night. 

As with the other convoy systems, you will be able to track your current allies and their requests on the Wayfarer’s Convoy page.

# HUMANITY’S LAST HOPE: NARRATIVE AND MECHANICAL OVERHAULS

One of the core design pillars of Campaign II, is to ensure that players have more meaningful agency and control over the unfolding narrative. Presenting challenging dilemmas that can dramatically impact the expedition’s journey. 

Our intention with Aeturnum has always been to provide an immersive and narrative-focused drop-in experience that rewards the emotional investment of players. Due to the nature of the format, drop-in games often struggle to capture the same chaotic reactivity that can make home games so special. 

Campaign I struggled to maintain this balance, with plot-essential NPCs and scripted scenes doing a lot of heavy lifting to ensure that the divergent narratives unfolding across five separate tables still cohered at the end of every special. 

With Campaign II and its gameplay systems, we are embracing the tension that comes with consequence. Casting the more linear and restrictive elements of Campaign I’s narrative framework aside to provide a player-driven experience that puts you and your actions in the spotlight. This is your journey, and we want to reflect that - ensuring that you are driving the action, instead of being a spectator to it. 

For our monthly Narrative Events, the pre-scripted scenes that would typically bookend a special will be replaced with the Hunter’s Council. A player from each table will be called forward and asked to decide how to approach that month’s expedition, and how the convoy should allocate its resources. Shifting your odds of survival, and your reputation across the Isles. 

It will be up to you to decide which allies should be brought with you into the field and which should remain behind. Whilst some might prove to be valuable assets on the battlefield, if killed you have the potential to lose the unique upgrades they might have provided the convoy.
Each month will see the Wayfarer’s Convoy arrive at a new destination, providing a self-contained arc that weaves into and drives forward the overarching narrative. These shorter mini-arcs will allow us to react to player choices more dynamically.  Providing a less daunting experience for new players who join later in the campaign, without compromising our focus on immersive storytelling. 

Your journey will take you across the entirety of the Red Isles - exploring new locations as we expand and dive deeper into the ruined world of Aeturnum beyond Adrestia’s shores.

# NEW PLAYER EXPERIENCE 

Ensuring that Aeturnum II is accessible to new players, and remains so throughout the course of the campaign is another core design pillar. Beyond our narrative overhauls, we intend to organically introduce these new systems within the new Wayfarer’s Convoy page and Prologue module. 

As part of the site overhaul, video tutorials will be available for all of these new mechanics, including an extended deep-dive for those looking to master this new arsenal. 

Key quest items and leads will be available to track via the Wayfarer’s Codex, along with lore entries for key discoveries. Further video updates and patch notes will be released as we expand and refine these new systems throughout your journey in Aeturnum II.
