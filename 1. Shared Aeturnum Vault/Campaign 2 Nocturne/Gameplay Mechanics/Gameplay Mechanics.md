# Afflictions

When a PC is killed, their player will be given the option between losing their character and creating a new one, or bringing their character back from the dead. If they choose to bring their character back, the PC will gain an affliction - a permanent debuff that may cause their entire gameplay style to change. 

A PC can be revived twice via this method, before the damage to their body is too great. At this point, the player must make a new character in order to continue playing.
# Bleed and Haemorrhage

Text from official source, as of 19/06/26;

Haemorrhage is a condition unique to campaign 2, typically inflicted by vampiric enemies and those carrying the Outer God of Blood's Curse.

Certain attacks and abilities will cause players to accumulate stacks of Haemorrhage. Once the number of Stacks reach their Haemorrhage Limit (Hit Dice + CON Modifier), the Haemorrhage effect will automatically trigger, as their wounds laced with curseblood split open.

The damage inflicted will be equivalent to the number of stacks of Haemorrhage accumulated when the effect is triggered. For each stack of Haemorrhage, they will take 1D6 Bleed Damage. Unless specified otherwise, this damage will not include stacks that exceed their total Haemorrhage Limit.

Once per round, a stack of Haemorrhage is automatically removed at the end of a player's turn. If a player receives healing from a single source equivalent to their Haemorrhage limit, they may roll their hit dice and remove that number of stacks.

The Haemorrhage condition is considered to be a curse. All accumulated stacks can be removed by any spell or action that would remove the curse condition. 
# Collecting Sovereigns, Lyria, and Ether

In order to upgrade the Convoy and our Hunter's Tools, we need to collect resources that can be found in various ways during quest boards.
- Sovereigns - The currency of the Red Isles, typically rewarded for completing a quest
- [[Technology#Lyria|Lyria]] - A magical substance, increasingly rare, typically found by completing optional objectives during a quest
- [[Technology#Etheric Cells|Ether]] - A magical energy source, incredibly rare, sometimes found by completing optional objectives

On the Arcanist's Tavern website, the [Wayfarer's Convoy](https://arcaniststavern.uk/wayfarersconvoy) page displays our current supply of all three materials, along with the projects that we can spend them on. 
# Equipment

A PC begins the campaign with all the equipment that is included with the 'starting equipment' options of their character build. They can also take up to two pieces of equipment listed on the [Wayfarer's Convoy](https://arcaniststavern.uk/wayfarersconvoy) section of the Arcanist's Tavern website. More equipment can be unlocked by completing the projects listed on the same page.
# Hunter's Journal

The booklet that a player receives after attending the prologue. It tracks their character level and their unlocked [[#Hunter's Tools]]. 
# Hunter's Tools

Devices used by the Wayfarer's Guild that offer a variety of helpful extra abilities. The basic tools can be seen on the [Wayfarer's Convoy](https://arcaniststavern.uk/wayfarersconvoy) section of the Arcanist's Tavern website. As a PC gains experience, they'll be able to upgrade their tools and gain access to new tools via the [[#Hunter's Journal]].
